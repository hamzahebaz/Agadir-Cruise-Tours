A Local's Guide to Souk El Had

Master the art of bargaining, find quality argan products, and navigate the largest market in North Africa.

May 12, 2026 • Written by Abdou, Local Guide • 5 min read

Souk El Had is the beating heart of Agadir. Spanning over 13 hectares and featuring 12 gates and more than 6,000 individual stalls, it is the largest urban market in North Africa. For a cruise passenger, it can be both incredibly exciting and slightly overwhelming. Here is our insider guide to navigating the souk like a local.

1. Knowing the Layout (The Gates)

Because of its size, getting lost is part of the experience. However, knowing the gates helps you keep your bearings:

  • Gate 5 & 6 are the main entrance points for fresh fruits, vegetables, and household goods.
  • Gate 8 is the place to go if you are looking for authentic Moroccan spices, olives, and dates.
  • Gate 9 & 10 house the leather goods, handcrafted souvenirs, slippers (babouches), and brass lamps.

2. The Art of Bargaining (Dirhams vs. Euros)

Bargaining is a fundamental part of shopping in Moroccan souks. The initial price quoted is almost always double or triple what the vendor expects to receive:

  • Rule of Thumb: Counter-offer with about 40% to 50% of the initial price, then negotiate slowly until you reach a mutually agreeable middle ground (usually around 60% of the original quote).
  • Keep it Friendly: Bargaining is a social interaction. Smile, be respectful, and if the price isn't right, you can always walk away politely.
  • Currency: While most stalls will accept Euros, paying in Moroccan Dirhams (MAD) will yield better exchange rates and cheaper prices.

3. Sourcing Authentic Argan Oil

Agadir and the surrounding Souss valley are the native home of the argan tree. Souk El Had is packed with stalls selling cosmetic and culinary argan oil.

  • Quality Test: Authentic cosmetic argan oil should have a very faint nut scent or virtually no smell at all and absorbs quickly into the skin. If it smells strongly of toasted nuts, it is culinary argan oil (delicious for dipping bread, but not for skin/hair care!).

4. Must-Buy Souvenirs

If you want to take home a piece of Morocco, we recommend looking out for:

  • Moroccan Spices: Ras el Hanout, high-grade cumin, and saffron threads.
  • Leather Bags: Hand-stitched satchels and slippers made of goat or camel leather.
  • Babouches: Colorful leather slippers, comfortable for indoor wear.
  • Amlou: A delicious Moroccan spread made of ground almonds, argan oil, and honey.
